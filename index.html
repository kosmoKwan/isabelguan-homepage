About

I'm a Lecturer at HKUST, where I designed and teach EESM 6000F — Fundamentals of Embodied AI, the department's first graduate course on the topic. My research focuses on embodied AI for robotics: foundation models for manipulation, adaptive learning from human feedback, and the design of robot autonomy stacks.

I completed my Ph.D at Peking University on adaptive robot learning, with visiting research at NTU Singapore under Academician I-Ming Chen. Before that, I earned an M.Sc from Northwestern University and a B.S. from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. I was named on the Forbes China 30 Under 30 and Hurun Under 30s Entrepreneurial Leaders lists in 2020.

I currently serve as a reviewer for CVPR, NeurIPS, ICRA, ROBIO, SII, and others.

Research

Foundation Models for Robotics

Vision-language-action models for manipulation and locomotion — how large pretrained models translate into reliable robot policies in the real world.

VLA · Manipulation · Locomotion

Adaptive Learning from Human Feedback

Closing the loop between humans and robots — preference-based RL, max-entropy exploration, and human-in-the-loop adaptation. The core of my Ph.D thesis.

RLHF · Exploration · HRI

End-to-End Robot Autonomy

From perception to control as a single learned system. Recent work spans ego-centric self-driving (EgoFSD) and ROS2-native robot controllers for manufacturing.

E2E · Self-driving · ROS2
